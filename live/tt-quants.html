01 · What you get

The numbers behind the trades.

A standard Tradetron dashboard shows you a PnL line. TT Quants shows you why that line moves — which leg, which time of day, which regime, which broker fill cost you. Per-strategy. Per-instrument. Per-month.

[ 01 ]
Per-strategy attribution
Break every rupee of PnL down by strategy, leg, instrument and day of week. The one strategy that's actually carrying the book — and the three that look fine but quietly bleed — both surface here.
[ 02 ]
Drawdown decomposition
When the book draws down, which positions caused it? Was it correlated drawdown across legs, a single anomaly fill, or a regime shift? Drawdowns get a post-mortem, not a shrug.
[ 03 ]
True-Net PnL
Gross PnL minus brokerage, STT, exchange fees, GST, SEBI charges, stamp duty and measured slippage. The number your broker statement reconciles to — not the optimistic backtest number.
[ 04 ]
Regime-conditional performance
"Strategy X earns ₹0.8 Sharpe in trending-up regimes, -0.2 in mean-reverting ones." Performance sliced by VIX bucket, trend strength, and realised-vs-implied vol — so you know which environments to scale up and which to dial back.
[ 05 ]
Slippage analysis
Difference between signal price, expected fill and actual fill — per broker, per time of day, per instrument. Catches the broker / time-of-day combinations where slippage quietly eats your edge.
[ 06 ]
Custom dashboards & exports
Pull whatever cut you need — by underlying, by author, by execution mode, by broker (Zerodha vs Upstox vs Angel One). Export to PDF or Excel for the LP, the auditor, or the monthly review.
02 · True-Net PnL

We show you what your broker won't.

Most platforms quote gross PnL and stop. Brokers tell you their charges line-by-line but won't sum it back against strategy performance. TT Quants does the join — every charge, every slippage rupee, against the strategy that incurred it.

Across a year of trading, the gap between "what the chart shows" and "what hit your bank account" is usually bigger than anyone admits. We make it visible.
